Not at all. The program starts with a 2-week foundation module that covers the math, electronics, and logic basics you need. It's designed specifically for people with zero robotics background — career changers, recent grads, and veterans all start here.

It's a blended format: roughly 60% self-paced online content and 40% in-person lab sessions. Online modules cover theory and demonstrations you can work through on your schedule. Lab sessions are where you get hands-on with real robots, PLCs, and industrial equipment in Walton County.

You'll earn your OSHA 10 General Industry certification and the RoboVoTech AI-Augmented Robotics Technician credential. The program also prepares you for FANUC CERT, Siemens SMSCP, and MSSC CPT — stackable industry certs that can earn you college credit through Florida's Gold Standard articulation system.
